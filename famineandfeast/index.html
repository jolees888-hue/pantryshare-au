The situation

This is not a drill.

Global fuel prices are driving up the cost of transporting food across Australia. Regional communities are already feeling it. Families are making hard choices. The supermarket shelves that Australians take for granted are under real pressure.

Governments move slowly. Communities can move fast.

The solution

Neighbours feeding neighbours.

PantryShare AU is our free community food sharing app — built for Australian suburbs. No sign-up required. No money changes hands. Just real people helping each other.

Step 01
Post what you have

List food you can share or swap — fresh produce, rice, pasta, tins, dairy, bread. Anything helps someone.

Step 02
Search your suburb

Browse listings by suburb or postcode. See what your neighbours have available right now.

Step 03
Connect directly

Contact each other by email. Arrange collection at your doorstep. No middleman, no fees, no fuss.

Why it matters

Built for the times we're in.

Floods. Cyclones. Rising costs. Conflict. Supply disruptions. Australia has always faced tough moments — and every time, it's been communities, not governments, that hold people together first. PantryShare AU exists to make that community network stronger, suburb by suburb, before any crisis hits.

️

Any emergency. Any suburb.

Whether it's fuel prices strangling supply chains, a cyclone cutting off a regional town, floodwaters isolating families, or global events disrupting what arrives on the shelf — the answer is the same: know your neighbours, share what you have, ask for what you need. PantryShare AU is your local food network, ready before the emergency arrives.

️

Community is the infrastructure.

Emergency services respond. Governments fund recovery. But in the hours, days and weeks in between — it's the people next door who show up with a bag of groceries. This app is about rebuilding that instinct. Knowing your street. Trusting your suburb. That connection is worth more than any stockpile.

"This is just neighbours looking out for each other."

Asking is part of how it works.

Life gets complicated for everyone — a rough week, a tight month, a disaster that changes things overnight. Posting a request on PantryShare AU is the same as texting a mate to ask if they have any extra pasta. Completely normal. Completely human. No forms, no eligibility, no explanations needed. Just people helping people — the way Australians always have.
